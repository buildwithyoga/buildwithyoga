The problem many yoga teachers face

Yoga teachers — people devoted to service (seva) — are often stuck with outdated websites, or locked into recurring subscription fees just to keep a basic page alive. Many pay month after month for tools that barely reflect the depth of what they teach.

That's the opposite of yoga's spirit of freedom and non-attachment (aparigraha). Your practice teaches you to let go of what doesn't serve you — your website shouldn't be one more thing quietly draining your energy and income every month.

Bringing technology and yoga together

After 7+ years in software engineering, I completed my M.Sc. in Yoga Therapy from S-VYASA. It was a journey that changed how I see both fields — technology isn't just about efficiency, and yoga isn't just about postures. Both, at their best, are about creating the right conditions for growth.

So I started building websites made specifically for the yoga community — ones you truly own, with no strings attached, and no dependency on me or anyone else to keep them running.
